# Fighter Advancement

Throughout a campaign, fighters will gain addition Gear which will boost their performance, but they can also gain improvements unlocking additional abilities and boosting their characteristics. Advancements can only be taken in the Aftermath sequence of a battle and each Fighter can only be given one Advancement each Aftermath.

## Talents

Talents are Abilities which can be purchased for Fighters, typically only Leaders and Champions. They do cost Points to add them to your Fighter but they do not increase the cost of the Fighter. So they are a good way to improve your Fighter without needing to cut points elsewhere on the Warband.

The cost for a Talent is 20 points for each talent a Fighter will have after this is purchased. So the first talent will cost 20, the second talent will cost 40, the third talent will cost 60 and so on.


| Name | M | WS | BS | Def | W | Traits | Cost |
| :---- | :---: | :---: | :---: | :---: | :---: | :--- | :---: |
| **Starting Captain** | 4 | 4 | 4 | 4 | 20 | Leader | 75 |
| **Talented Captain** | 4 | 4 | 4 | 4 | 20 | Leader, Sprint, Quick Shot | 75 |

:::info Philosophy
An experience system was considered but ultimately was rejected to keep things simple. This way we have only a single resource for the Rosters making it easier to track. The talents are intended to be Abilities so they will be locked under the Ability Dice system.
:::


## Characteristic boosts

Like Talents, Characteristic boosts cost points. In this case it may reflect money spent on training. Unlike with Talents this does count towards the overall cost of the Fighter so it will increase the cost of the Fighter for Missions. Not all Characteristics can be boosted, only Weapon Skill, Ballistic Skill, Defense, and Wounds. The first advance for a characteristic costs 10 points with the second advance costing 20 points. Each Characteristic can only be advanced twice. For Wounds, each advance costs 10 and gives an additional 2 wounds.

| Name | M | WS | BS | Def | W | Traits | Cost |
| :---- | :---: | :---: | :---: | :---: | :---: | :---: | :---: |
| **Starting Captain** | 4 | 4 | 4 | 4 | 20 | Leader | 75 |
| **Advanced Captain** | 4 | 6 | 6 | 6 | 24 | Leader | 185 |

:::info Philosophy
If we want to try balance missions we need to include characteristic boosts.
:::


## Talent List

Here are a list of Talent options which can be used in a campaign. Universes may add additional Talents.

### Shooting Talents

| Name | Cost | Effect |
| :---- | :---: | :---- |
| <b id="quick-shot">Quick Shot</b> | [double] | This fighter may make a bonus ranged attack action with one fewer attack die. This action must target a visible enemy within half the weapon’s maximum range. |
| <b id="pistolier">Pistolier</b> | [double] | For this fighter's next attack they may target two different enemies within 6" when using a paired set of ranged weapons, reducing the dual weapon’s attacks by 1, and rolling separately for each target. |
| <b id="eagle-eye">Eagle Eye</b> | [double] | Add 5" to the maximum range of this fighter’s next ranged attack action this turn. |
| <b id="trick-shooter">Trick Shooter</b> | [double] | For the next ranged attack, line of sight does not have to be a straight line. Use the new "curved" path to check both visibility and total range. |
| <b id="aimed-shot">Aimed Shot</b> | [triple] | If this fighter has not moved this round, they gain +2 Ballistic Skill on their next ranged attack and cannot take move actions for the remainder of this turn. |
| <b id="marksman">Marksman</b> | [double] | For the fighter's next attack with a ranged weapon, if it would normally crit on a 6 it now also crits on a 5. |
