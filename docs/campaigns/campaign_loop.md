# Campaign Loop

## Campaign Loop

Each game follows a basic sequence:

1. Select Fighters from the Roster to make a Warband.
2. Play a battle with the Warband.
3. Resolve the aftermath sequence.
4. Update your Roster.

This loop repeats for the duration of the campaign.

---

## Campaign Treasure

Alter the 6.2 Battlefield Setup step to add three **Campaign Treasure** tokens to the battlefield. As this is placed before the Deployment has been drawn, both players should be able to agree locations that make sense. These are treated as normal [treasure tokens](../core-rules/core_rules_6/core_rules_6_3#treasure-rules) but do not count for the purposes of Victory conditions. At the end of the Mission, Fighters holding one of these three treasure tokens generate 10 points for their player's Roster.

---

## Campaign Phases

At the start of a Campaign Warbands will consist of 1000 points worth of Fighters and Equipment. This can be played for several games while Rosters grow, but eventually players will wish to up the points limit to allow them bring more elite forces.

After each game, a player will gain points, 100 for winning 50 for losing and some treasure from missions. There's a decent chance players will lose a Fighter each battle so they will lose some points from their Roster. As a guideline, increase the Warband size by 200 for each 5 games in a campaign. If a player is unable to field a Warband at the current phase size due to a lack of points in their roster, they should work out the differnce and recruit Fighters and Gear for the Warband up to the cost of that difference. At the end of that battle, those Fighters and Gear are removed and do not get added to the Player's Roster.
