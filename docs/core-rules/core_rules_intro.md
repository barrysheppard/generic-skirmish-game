# Introduction

## 1.0 Overview {#1.0-overview}

Generic Skirmish Game is a skirmish level tabletop miniatures game. Players control Warbands of 3 to 15 fighters as they battle it out. This all takes place on your table top with the battlefield represented with scenery and the fighters with miniatures. Games are played out over four turns with a single roll attack system which keeps the action quick and exciting.

## 1.1 Game Components {#1.1-game-components}

- **Miniatures** – 3 to 15 models per side to represent fighters, these will typically be 28mm to 32mm scale with bases between 25mm and 40mm. While round bases are preferable, square bases can work fine.
- **Board** – This game is played on a 30" by 22" board by default, but can be played on other board sizes.
- **Fighter profiles** – Each fighter has a profile showing their stats, weapons, and abilities. This may be represented on a page or as a combination of cards combined together (e.g. a fighter card, a weapon card, a gear card).
- **Mission** – The mission describes how the battle will be played, where fighters start, and how a winner will be determined. It is composed of three elements:  
  - **Deployment**: Where fighters start on the board and what turn they arrive.
  - **Victory**: How the winning Warband is determined at the end of the game.
  - **Twist**: Any special rules in effect for this game.
- **Dice** – This game uses multiple six sided dice, around 12 per person is good.
- **Tokens** – It is important to track wounds and which fighters have activated, so tokens for this will really help. Activation markers with a line on one side (one action used) and a cross on the other (two actions used) work best.
- **Measuring Tools** – Distances in this game are measured in inches.

## 1.2 General Principles {#1.2-general-principles}

All dice in this game are six sided dice (D6).
- For a D3 roll a D6 and results of 1-2 are considered 1, 3-4 are considered 2, and 5-6 are considered 3.
- For a D66 roll, roll two D6 in order (either one at a time or use two different coloured onces and designate one colour as the first). Results are recorded toghter as a single number, e.g. 11, 12, 13, ... 45, 46, 51, ... 65, 66. This gives a total of 36 possible results.
- For a roll of 2D6 roll two D6 and add the results, this will give a value from 2 to 12.

Distances are measured in inches.
- When a fighter moves, measure from a single point on its base to that same point in its final position. The fighter’s facing cannot change during this move.
- Range between Fighters is measured between the closest points on each base.


## 1.3 Game Inspiration {#1.3-game-inspiration}

This game is heavily inspired by the Games Workshop game Warcry with it's single roll attack system.
