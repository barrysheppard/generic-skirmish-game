# Fighter Selection

## 6.4 Fighter Selection

Now the Battlefield has been set up and the Victory condition determined, players select Fighters for their Warband from their Roster. This will allow a player to make some strategic decisions to help achieve their objective. Depending on the setting, this may include selecting specialists who are good at specific tasks, for example a Thief to get through locked doors or a Hacker to break into a computer vault.

:::warning Change from Warcry
This is a change to the normal order where Warbands would be selected without knowing the Victory condition or the Battlefield. This change is to hopefully add some decision points into Warband selection and will also allow Campaign play to include Specialists which you might leave behind for most missions but bring along for key ones.
:::


When selecting a Fighter from the Roster, you can choose to not take some Weapons or Gear to reduce the points of the Fighter.

If a Fighter has more than one Weapon, the points for the most expensive Weappon is treated as normal but additional weapons are reduced to 10 for the purposes of Warband selection.

:::info Philosophy
A Fighter can only use one Weapon profile during an attack. Additional Weapons are useful options to have but reducing them to a minial cost is best for balance.
:::


After fighters are selected for the Warband, they are divided as evenly as possible into three deployment groups of A, B, and C. This is divided by numbers of fighters and not by points.

When dividing your fighters into groups, it is assumed that A will be the primary group with groups B and C as secondary groups. You include fighters you want working together in the same Deployment Group. Missions will reference Deployment Groups, especially in the Deployment details, which you may wish to consider.


:::info Philosophy
Splitting the Warband into groups speeds up the game by simplifying the deployment. Fighters are deployed together to locations defined in the deployment layout. This diverges for the 'normal' approach of a back and forth of deployment units that in some games can define the winner before the first shot is fired.
:::
