# Other actions


## 7.7 Wait Action

<TraitGallery include={["Wait"]} showCost={false} />

A fighter gets two actions each turn. If for their first action they take a **Wait** action, they may end their activation and are given a wait token. This indicates they have taken 1 action and have 1 action remaining. A fighter who already has a wait token who takes a wait action replaces the wait token with an acted token instead.

Typically a Wait token is indicated with a single line and an Acted token is two lines crossed. The Wait token indicates 1 action has been taken, an Acted token indicates all two actions have been taken.

A fighter with a wait token can be activated later in the round at which point they can take that remaining action. This **Wait** action is a way for a Warband to control tempo within the game by trading an action to force their opponent to act first.

## 7.8 Ability Actions

<TraitGallery include={["Lever"]} showCost={false} />

Some missions, special rules, or equipment may allow a character to trade an action for an effect, for example pulling a level to open a door. These are presented as abilities with the [action] cost. Unless stated otherwise they follow the normal rules for abilities, i.e. they can only be activated during a Fighter's activation unless the ability is a Reaction.



## 7.9 Stealth Action

If a fighter is not visible to any enemy fighter, they may take the **Stealth action** to gain a **Stealth token**.

<TraitGallery include={["Stealth"]} showCost={false} />


While they have this token:

* They cannot be targeted by attacks or abilities unless the attacker can draw an unobstructed line of sight (i.e., no intervening cover).
* Enemy fighters cannot react to actions of this fighter.
* At the end of a fighter’s activation they lose the token if they:
  * Made an attack with a weapon (that doesn't have the Silent trait) this activation, or
  * they are visible to an enemy fighter within 6", or
  * they are visible to an enemy fighter with an unobstructed line of sight.

A fighter only loses their Stealth token at the end of their activation or due to another ability effect.


:::info Philosophy
The stealth action is there to give the option for modern day tactical game play. In a setting where range attacks are weaker and melee is the focus, the Stealth action is unlikely to be worthwhile. In a setting where ranged weapons are strong, the Stealth action is likely to be an important factor. By default the Stealth Action is a steep cost in a 4 turn game to gain stealth. It is expected that the Stealth token will also be generated in other ways such as using ability dice.
:::
