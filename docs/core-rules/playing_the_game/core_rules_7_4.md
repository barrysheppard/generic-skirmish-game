# Move Actions


## 7.4 Move Action

<TraitGallery include={["Move"]} showCost={false} />

Fighters cannot move through other fighters, through a terrain feature, or over a battlefield edge. If during a move, a fighter moves into melee range (within 1 inch) of an enemy fighter they must remain within 1 inch of that fighter for the remainder of the move. If the fighter starts their move within melee range (1 inch) of an enemy fighter, they must stay within melee range of that fighter for all of their move.

**Jumping** – Fighters may jump across horizontal gaps equal to or less than their Move. Falling mid-jump causes falling damage. 

**Climbing** – If a fighter has contact with a terrain surface (e.g. a wall) they may climb up that terrain. This can allow a fighter to move vertically up or down. A fighter can finish their move while still climbing, but if they finish their activation while climbing they will fall, moving directly down to the nearest platform or the battlefield floor.

**Falling** – If for any reason a fighter ends up not on the battlefield floor or a platform, they fall. The opposing player picks a point on a platform or battlefield floor that is lower than the falling fighter and is within 2" horizontally of the falling fighter. The centre of the fighters base is placed on that spot. If their new location is 2" or more below their original location, they may take impact damage. The owner of the fighter rolls a dice, on a 1 they take 3 damage, on a 2-3 they take 1 damage, on a 4+ they take no damage.

## 7.5 Disengage Action

<TraitGallery include={["Disengage"]} showCost={false} />

A fighter that is within 1" of another fighter is considered to be 'in melee'. A fighter in melee cannot make a normal move to leave melee and instead cautiously retreats using a **Disengage** action. For a **Disengage** a fighter can move up to 3" but must end up outside of melee range from all enemy fighters. If the acting fighter for some reason has a move of less than 3, they can move up to their full movement. Although the fighter does move a number of inches, for game purposes this is not considered a movement and does not trigger effects that impact or react to a move action.

Fighters cannot **Disengage** through other fighters, through a terrain feature, or over a battlefield edge.
