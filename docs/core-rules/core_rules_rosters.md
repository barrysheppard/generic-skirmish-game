# Rosters and Warbands

In a Campaign you will purchase Fighters and Gear and add them to your Roster. Then, for a Mission, you will select Fighters from your Roster to make up the Warband that will fight.

## 2.1 Rosters  {#2.1-Rosters}

For your first game (or a one-off game) your Roster and Warband will be the same as you'll start with just enough in your Roster to build a Warband. In campaigns Fighters in your Roster will gain advancements and gear to make them more effective. They may also become injured taking on negative traits and when they die they are removed from the Roster.

A starting Roster consists of a total of 1000 points worth of Fighters and Gear. 

## 2.2 Warband Composition {#2.2-Warband-composition}

For a game, each player must create a Warband from their Roster of Fighters.

The exact requirements for a Warband will depend on the setting and possibly Mission, but by default the following Warband construction rules apply:

- One fighter in the Warband must be the **Leader**, usually this will be the best fighter in the Warband.
- Up to 2 other fighters can have the **Champion** trait, these are powerful fighters similar to the **Leader**.
- Any number of other Fighters up to a maximum of 15 fighters total (including the leader and any champions).
- The total points of Fighters and Gear must be less than or equal to 1000 points.

Details of Warbands and Fighter profiles will be covered in separate setting documents.
