# Abilities


## Stealth Action {#stealth-action}

If a fighter is not visible to any enemy fighter, they may take the **Stealth action** to gain a **Stealth token**.

| Name | Ability Cost | Effect |
| :---- | :---- | :---- |
| **Stealth** | [action] | A fighter that cannot draw line of sight to an enemy fighter, can take this action to gain a stealth token. |

While they have this token:

* They cannot be targeted by attacks or abilities unless the attacker can draw an unobstructed line of sight (i.e., no intervening cover).
* Enemy fighters cannot react to actions of this fighter.
* At the end of a fighter’s activation they lose the token if they:
  * Made an attack with a weapon (that doesn't have the Silent trait) this activation, or
  * they are visible to an enemy fighter within 6", or
  * they are visible to an enemy fighter with an unobstructed line of sight.

A fighter only loses their Stealth token at the end of their activation or due to another ability effect.


:::info Philosophy
The stealth action is there to give the option for modern day tactical game play. In a setting where range attacks are weaker and melee is the focus, the Stealth action is unlikely to be worthwhile. In a setting where ranged weapons are strong, the Stealth action is likely to be an important factor. By default the Stealth Action is a steep cost in a 4 turn game to gain stealth. It is expected that the Stealth token will also be generated in other ways such as using ability dice.
:::
