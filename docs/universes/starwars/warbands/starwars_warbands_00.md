# Assembling your Squad {#assembling-your-squad}

Warbands in this format are referred to as squads. There are three available factions: Imperial, Rebels, and Scum with some fighters available to multiple factions. There is no requirement to have a leader or limitations on heroes. Profiles are split into Units and Characters. When selecting models from a unit, initially you will only have access to the first profile in the list. Taking more fighters from that unit will open up additional options. For example, you cannot just take a Stormtrooper with a Missile Launcher, instead if you take 4 basic Stormtroopers then the next Stormtrooper you take can have a Missile Launcher.

> *Philosophy: These units are based around the available boxes of models for Star Wars Legion. Profile limitations are included to give some sort of structure and weird looking lists that might spam all missile launchers. As fighters have costs, these limitations could be ignored without any major impact.*

Fighters do have a cost listed in credits (cr), that cost includes their weapons and gear. The current guideline is for 1000 credits per Warband, which will result in relatively small Warbands. A Rebel Operative is 120 credits, so 8 will cost 960 points. Similarly a Stormtrooper costs 145 points so 6 will cost 870\. There are some options and upgrades but as this setting has quite powerful ranged weapons, the credit costs will be quite high. Players should consider whether higher totals will make more sense, as long as each side has similar points and total fighters are 15 or less, then it should be fine.

After a Squad has been assembled it should be split into three Fire Warbands: A, B, and C. Fighters in each Fire Warband deploy together. It is assumed that Fire Warband A is your primary Fire Warband and the suggested deployments have 4 in which Warband A starts the game on the board. It would be a mistake to neglect Fire Warbands B and C however as in the remaining two deployments one of those two fire Warbands starts the game alone.
