### **Stealth Action** {#stealth-action}

If a fighter is not visible to any enemy fighter, they may take the **Stealth action** to gain a **Stealth token**. While they have this token:

* They cannot be targeted by attacks or abilities unless the attacker can draw an unobstructed line of sight (i.e., no intervening cover).
* At the end of a fighter’s activation they lose the token if they:  
  * Made an attack with a weapon this activation, or  
  * they are visible to an enemy fighter within 6", or  
  * they are visible to an enemy fighter with an unobstructed line of sight.

A fighter only loses their Stealth token at the end of their activation or due to another ability effect.

Philosophy: The stealth action is there to give the option for modern day tactical game play. In a setting where range attacks are weaker and melee is the focus, the Stealth action is unlikely to be worthwhile. In a setting where ranged weapons are strong, the Stealth action is likely to be an important factor.

Philosophy: Star Wars Legion is a token heavy game. When you buy Legion miniatures you end up with Tokens. As such, many of the abilities in this pack use tokens to indicate special effects. Agree at the start of the game which tokens relate to which effects.

## **Using Multiple Abilities** {#using-multiple-abilities}

In the default rules, only one ability can be used per activation. You cannot, for example, use an ability that increases your attack dice and another ability that gives you an extra attack. This made sense where there are generic abilities that everyone can use. In this setting pack a fighter does not automatically have access to generic abilities. As such, we can remove the restriction for a fighter to only use one ability per activation and instead say each ability can only be triggered once per activation (but the limit on reactions remains). When assigning abilities we do need to be careful to consider where stacking abilities could scale each other. As such, a Stormtrooper with a heavy blaster can trigger Rapid Fire for an extra dice on the attack and then trigger Suppressive Fire to debuff enemy fighters. We should avoid giving the Stormtroopers an ability that gives them an extra attack which would act as a scalar with the Rapid Fire extra dice.

## **Passive abilities** {#passive-abilities}

With this variant, all traits are treated as abilities. The abilities that do not require initiative dice and are always on are referred to as \[passive\] abilities. As they are always active, they do not fall into limitations on triggering abilities. They also cannot be turned off and some of these \[passive\] abilities are actually penalties.

| Name | Cost | Effect |
| :---- | ----- | :---- |
| **Beast** | \[passive\] | Cannot open doors, climb ladders, or use equipment. Often found on animal or creature-type fighters. |
| **Droid** | \[passive\] | A fighter with the Droid trait is an artificial, intelligent machine designed to perform a wide range of tasks. This trait has no direct effect but other abilities may reference the Droid trait. |
| **Fly** | \[passive\] | This fighter can move over terrain and other models without being impeded. They do not need to climb or go around obstacles when moving. |
| **Heavy** | \[passive\] | This weapon cannot be fired after moving this turn. |
| **Immobile** | \[passive\] | A fighter with this trait or with a weapon with this trait cannot make a move or disengage action. |
| **Non-Combatant** | \[passive\] | Enemy fighters cannot target this model with attacks or abilities if there is another closer visible target. This restriction is ignored if the enemy fighter is within 6” of this model. |
| **Reload** | \[passive\] | This weapon may only be fired once per turn. |
| **Scope** | \[passive\] | This weapon gains \+1 Weapon Skill if the shooter has not moved this turn (they can still move after shooting). |
| **Silent** | \[passive\] | Weapons with this trait do not break Stealth or trigger enemy Reactions when used. Usually attached to weapons but passive in effect. |
| **Stun** | \[passive\] | On a critical hit, the target loses an action if possible (gain a wait token or change a wait token to an activated token). |

## **Special Skills** {#special-skills}

The GSG rules focus on combat and action, but sometimes other skills are needed. For example, opening a sealed door might require a Tech skill roll. By default, fighters are considered to have a skill of 1, rolling 1 dice. Successes are determined in the same manner as an attack roll with each task having a difficulty.

Example Skills: Computers, Mechanic, Medicine, Deception, Linguistics.

For example, if accessing data on a terminal required a Computers (2) task. An unskilled individual would roll a D6 and compare their skill of 1 versus the task of 2 and would need a 5+ to hit with no chance to crit. A fighter with a Computers (2) skill could have to roll a 4+ to hit and could crit. Often critting won’t be relevant but some tests might have additional bonuses.

Some gear may give a fighter a skill or may give extra dice.
